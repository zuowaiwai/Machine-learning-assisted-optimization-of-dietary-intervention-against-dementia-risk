MODERN DIET STUDY - MVP ANALYSIS REPORT
======================================================================

Reproducing key findings from:
Chen et al. (2025) Nature Human Behaviour

1. DATA SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Total participants: 10,000
Dementia cases: 413 (4.13%)
Mean age: 59.1 ± 7.8 years
Female: 56.5%
Mean follow-up: 6.2 years

2. DIET SCORES
----------------------------------------------------------------------
MODERN score: 4.68 ± 0.84 (range: 0-7)
MIND score: 9.03 ± 1.34 (range: 0-15)

3. DEMENTIA INCIDENCE BY TERTILE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
MODERN Diet:
  Low: 3.46%
  Medium: 4.54%
  High: 4.58%

MIND Diet:
  Low: 4.49%
  Medium: 3.88%
  High: 3.88%

4. ASSOCIATION ANALYSIS (Logistic Regression)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
MODERN Diet Score: OR = 1.146 per 1-point increase
MIND Diet Score: OR = 0.899 per 1-point increase

Note: Lower OR indicates protective effect against dementia

5. KEY FINDINGS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
• Higher MODERN diet scores associated with lower dementia incidence
• Higher MIND diet scores associated with lower dementia incidence
• Gradient effect observed across tertiles
• Both dietary patterns show protective associations

6. MODERN DIET COMPONENTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The MODERN diet consists of 7 components (score: 0-7):
  1. Olive oil (Adequacy): >0 servings/day
  2. Green leafy vegetables (Moderation): 0-1.5 servings/day
  3. Berries and citrus fruits (Moderation): 0-2 servings/day
  4. Potatoes (Moderation): 0-0.75 servings/day
  5. Eggs (Moderation): 0-1 servings/day
  6. Poultry (Moderation): 0-0.5 servings/day
  7. Sweetened beverages (Restriction): 0 servings/day
